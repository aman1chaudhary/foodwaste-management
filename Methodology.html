METHODOLOGY

The methodology involved in making the matrix:-

  1. Identification of the input to be added by the users. (Quantity of raw materials used, Weight of food wasted, Number of plates used)
  2. The raw material used per person is calculated and its corresponding weight is used to calculate its portion in the total wasted food.
  3. The portion of each raw material wasted is then multiplied by their respective footprints and then the respective value of footprints obtained for all raw materials are summed up to provide the total footprint of the meal.

WATER FOOTPRINT

The water footprint is a measure of humanity’s appropriation of fresh water in volumes of water consumed and/or polluted.


CARBON FOOTPRINT

Food’s carbon footprint, or foodprint, is the greenhouse gas emissions produced by growing, rearing, farming, processing, transporting, storing, cooking and disposing of the food you eat.

ASSUMPTIONS

  1. The footprint of each food item is calculated based on data from different locations and varied climatic conditions.
  2. The methods of calculating respective footprint and yield of each item is also based on different methods and data based on different factors such as location, climatic conditions and season.
  3. In case where data was provided in range of values, the input value used for such data is calculated by finding the average of the range.
  4. Energy and labour work involved in cooking has not been considered, since we do not have viable source of data available.
  5. Waste has been categorized in accordance with the food waste data collected from the mess on the basis of per plate serve.
  6. The process by which a particular food product was made, involves various small contributions by intermediate processes to the overall footprint value. These contributions are not being calculated towards final footprint value.

DATA SOURCES

Data from numerous national and international statistical surveys have been used to assess water footprint, carbon footprint, energy footprint, and labour footprint.
